  • Skylight

    This piece came from a spark of inspiration upon looking at an overcast sunset sky one day, as the clouds broke letting out rays of light. This inspired the lyrics and melody of this piece, Skylight. I used this song as an opportunity to explore and talk about my feelings when composing, and the idea of how inspiration can hit you all at once, and then suddenly disappear, leaving you with writer's block. I hope you all can relate to some aspects of this music’s story.

  • Forest Fantasie

    Forest Fantasie takes you on a trip through various characteristics of a forest environment, from frolicking about at the start, to wandering the mysterious ancient parts. This is then interrupted by chaotic rhythms that manipulate the original themes of the piece as nature is disturbed by outside influences.

    Titlescreen


    I composed this soundtrack for a music-focused roguelike created for a game jam, where as you play the game and earn more abilities, more instruments get added to the track. I was the lead programmer and composer, alongside a team of 8 other people. Our game was considered one of the best sounding masterpieces submitted to their competition.

    Feast or Famine

    Feast!

    Famine!


    I composed this soundtrack for a tower defense-side scrolling game I worked on with 11 other people as composer and one of 3 programmers. I based the style of the Feast! track on the Central African Soukous genre of music, and the Famine! track on the polyrhythmic hunting music of the Pygmies. This game tied for first place in a game jam I was a part of, along with winning awards for "Best Narrative", "Emotional Masterpiece", and "Most Innovative".

    Link to the game on Itch.io
